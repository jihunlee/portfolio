P & G

Product Design Project

I am not allowed to talk about details of the project due to non disclosure agreement

We were asked by P&G to redesign a package of one of their most famous consumer product

Personal Contributions to the project

In Home Visit

Extracted pain points from user behaviors by observing and analyzing user-shadowing videos. I was able to discover pain points by pointing out specific body cues and facial expression of users while performing their daily use of the product. Biggest challenge of the process was that users internalize their pain points especially due to the nature of the product (Because users have used the product almost everyday for years, they are so used to the pain points and developed workarounds, they were not even aware they still faced many challenges dealing with the product)



Contextual Interview

Formulated an interview guide by following guideline learned from Consumer Insight class to gain unmet needs and pain points. (For example, one of the main rules of formulating interview guideline is interviewer not asking direct questions about the product. Because we want to understand users’ views not only on the product but also on users’ personal aspiration and their unmet needs, it is always better that interviewer carefully guide interviewee so that he or she naturally brings out the topic.)



Framework / Design direction

Created links between overarching framework of unmet needs and specific pain points of users. I proposed to filter initially discovered pain points down to three by comparing the relevance to the overarching framework..



Ideation / Prototype / User Testing

Generated ideas and created prototypes to test with users. I evaluated our solutions first by checking whether they satisfied the design direction, and secondly analyzing if the solutions satisfied Don Norman’s three aspect of design (visceral, emotional, and aspirational).
